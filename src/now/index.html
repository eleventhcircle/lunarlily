Nonfiction I'm reading

Slowly working through another web-related history: The Two Revolutions: A History of the Transgender Internet, by Avery Dame-Griff. It's an interesting dive into how the transgender community of the 1980s and 90s shaped the Internet - and how our community was shaped by it in turn, for better or worse. I suggested it as a purchase to my library last year and was pleasantly surprised when I got the confirmation email! I'm a slow nonfiction reader so I'm not sure if I'll get through all of it before it's due back to the library but I can always check it out again lol

Stationery I'm using

Zebra Sarasa gel pens have been my ride or die for an age and they're still the first thing I reach for in the morning when I crack open my planner. I haven't bothered with stickers or washi tape in there for a while, but using colored inks still gives a little visual fun without much extra work yknow? Big into the milk color series right now, they're pastel but still very easy to read.

Now onto a couple recent additions to my collection: I recently had to invest in a new correction tape! Tombow no longer makes the model I bought in high school (the case had an eraser built in which saved some room in my pencil case) and I used up my last refill so I had to find a new one. I stuck with Tombow because I find their stuff to be quite reliable, just switched to the newer Mono Air refillable model. The tape lays down sooooo smooth, it's awesome :]

For Christmas I got myself a gift: a TWSBI ECO fountain pen, in white with silver hardware and an EF nib. It's my first "fancy" (read: over $25) pen and I am absolutely in love with how it writes!! Right now I have it inked with Robert Oster's Deep Sea, another Christmas gift from my partner this time. It's such a beautiful green-blue. Fairly well behaved too, though it likes to bleed on really cheap paper. I keep fishing for excuses to use this pen beyond journaling...
