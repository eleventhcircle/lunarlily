On the Friday before last, I got together with a little group of pals to try out a new (to us) party game called Guilty As Sock (unofficial review: infinite potential for shenanigans in there, will play again). There wasn't anything really different or remarkable about this hangout, maybe beyond the fact that it was the first time we'd been able to all get together in a while. But even that in itself isn't too strange; we've always had A Time getting our schedules to line up. The joys of adulthood and all that.

So no, Friday wasn't anything special. But it was important to me nonetheless.

Despite my resolution sometime last year to become a more social creature, I instead became quite the hermit. In the interest of privacy I won't go into a lot detail, but it's like... everything just became Too Much, y'know? Work got crazy busy and life just kept throwing one emergency after another at my family...  After a while, all I could do was try to keep my head above water. There was no time or energy to spare for anything else. I don't think I could tell you when I last spent time with a friend before that Friday - I really don't remember. It's been months, for sure. Maybe not even since sometime last year...? I've also barely spoken to anyone I care about in almost as long, instead retreating further into my shell the more that things got worse. And for lack of a more eloquent way to put this: that was a really dumb thing for me to do! Feeling so isolated made the rest of it seem that much worse.

As with all the bad times in life, though, it's not gonna last forever. Things are slowly getting better again! Or at least... more manageable. Work is slowing down to a normal pace, most life emergencies are under control, and I'm getting an almost respectable amount of sleep every night.

I wanted to connect again to my friends who I missed so much, but I also got deeply (and unnecessarily) in my head about how hermit-y I'd become. I stayed squirreled away for several more weeks. I was so scared that everyone would hate me and throw tomatoes at me for going radio silent that I couldn't bring myself to actually reach out now, despite really! fucking!! wanting to!!! And like... obviously no tomatoes were thrown lol. That's just not what friends do to each other, even if the little anxiety goblins in your head try to convince you otherwise. Once I started yapping in the group chat again, it was like I was never gone in the first place. Hanging out in call felt just like any other time. Hell, we've even gotten together again since then and that went perfectly fine too! Everyone was just happy to have me there, and I was happy to be there with them. I've been feeling better mentally in the last week or so than I have in many months and I'm a little chagrined to know that I could've felt like this a lot sooner, if I had gotten out of my own way. So I suppose the moral of this story is: sometimes you need a couple hours of improv and goofing with your friends to remind you why you keep going. Prommy they won't bring tomatoes.

I'm grateful to all of my friends who are so endlessly understanding when I shut myself away like this, patient and welcoming even as they weather their own hardships. It's a gift to feel like I always have somewhere to come home to. I hope you can find a place like that, too :]

We laughed so much in just a couple hours that my throat hurt something fierce the next day. I think that's the sweetest ache in the world.
