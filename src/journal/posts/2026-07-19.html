On July 3, 2025, a website was born!

I was a bit surprised when I checked the calendar a couple days ago and realized a year had already drifted by... so many things have happened, yet it feels like it passed in a blink. A single year took an eternity to live through when I was younger, but as I get closer to 30 I feel like time moves much faster. Y'know what I mean? It's as though, if I'm not careful, it'll all pass me by without even realizing. The days and weeks can blend into each other so easily now, if I have nothing to differentiate them or look forward to... Maybe this is how it feels to settle into the routines of adulthood? It's comforting and a little disconcerting at the same time.

But this is meant to be a reflection on the site getting older, not me lol

This is the longest I've stuck with a single project... ever? Or in a very long time, at least. I'm really proud of myself for that; I'm usually someone who bounces between lots of things, returning to any given project sporadically if at all, so this is a refreshing change of pace. In just a year I yapped about all sorts of things in the journal (totaling to just over 8k words! holy shit!), I learned how to use an SSG, I created a whole new site design all by myself... I even bought my very first domain! Wowie! I actually accomplished more than I thought, now that I'm looking back through the changelogs. Kinda nuts.

I focused a lot on removing the annoyances that put me off wanting to work on the site at all. Obviously the very act of updating the site itself was a pain point for a while, hence my migration to Eleventy. Another frustration revealed itself with time: I found myself lamenting my inability to work on things when not actively sitting at my desk, especially since I'm someone who ends up stuck in bed from pain or fatigue several times a month. At first I tried just using my tablet as a scribble pad in bed. Very good when I wanted to yap in a journal entry! Less good when I wanted to work on anything more technical. So in May(ish) I dug out my old college laptop to see if she had any life left in her. After purging Windows and setting up on Linux, it's been working surprisingly well! I've got VS Codium and Obsidian on here so I can work on writing or coding as I please, and git keeps all my work synced between the laptop and my main PC. The hardware isn't strong enough to handle much in the way of gaming (she was already far from cutting edge when I bought her, and now we're 10 years on...) so it's tough to get distracted from working on sitely things because I don't have much else installed lol.

In keeping with my tradition of celestial names for electronics, her name is Andromeda. She has a built-in optical drive which tickles me endlessly.

There's something so nostalgic about surfing the web or scrolling Tumblr on her. We really lost something as a society when we moved on from laptop in bed, man... phone in bed doesn't hit the same.

I'm surprised by just how much lunarlily affected my creativity overall. I figured I could keep my interest up in the site itself for most of the year, but I didn't expect it to put the wind back in my sails for other projects too! I picked crochet back up, I started writing and playing dolls with my OCs again, I got back into journaling regularly... I'm even in the planning stages for a new cosplay, something I haven't done since college! It feels good to want to make things again. Finding the time and energy to be creative is still its own struggle, but I'm happy that I even want to try in the first place.

In hindsight, though, I wasn't as consistent about updating the site as I thought I'd be. Weekly seemed like a reasonable update cadence at the beginning, but I've since come to realize that I just don't have the kind of time required to make that work. And that's okay! Lesson learned :] For the next year, my new goal will be to share something new roughly every month instead. That way I can spend time on other hobbies without feeling guilty for neglecting webweaving, and when life gets hectic I won't feel pressured to be creative when I really just want to potato in bed for a bit.

I've got no shortage of ideas for things I want to make for lunarlily! My current project is a guide to making a personal website, targeted specifically at my friends who have asked me multiple times how they can get started. (It makes my heart grow three sizes each time I'm asked btw, thank you so much.) I'm a slow writer but I work on it on my laptop before bed most nights, so it's coming along at a pretty decent pace (for me, anyway). I really want it to be something helpful for the people I'm writing it for. This is a hobby that I've come to really love, a way to express myself that I've never really had before. If I can convince even just one person I love to give webweaving a try, I would be over the moon with joy... Of course, if anyone else out there stumbles upon the guide and ends up getting some mileage out of it too, all the better!

Once that's done... to be honest, I'm not sure what my next project will be. It's hard picking one thing to work on when everything sounds so exciting.

I'm plagued by a plethora of ideas and not enough free time to make them all real. You can get a sneak peek of upcoming nav bar changes if you zoom in, but the rest is a secret!

My partner has been my biggest cheerleader by far this year. He loves to tell people I have a website; sometimes he feels like a proud father bragging about his kid lol. I use him as a rubber ducky a lot when I get stuck or frustrated, and he's always so warmly encouraging when I get insecure about what I'm making... I really don't think I would have stuck to it if he wasn't there to give me those little boosts. (and also to force me off Endfield so I would actually open VSC lol) Thank you Addy ilu mwah :]

I thought it might be a fun exercise to jot down some thoughts of things I want to do or try with the site. We can look back in another year and see what actually came to fruition. Don't take this as a set in stone list! More just me brainstorming a bit with the garage door open.

  • I want to be a little braver about sharing my opinions on things. Like, I have a lot of thoughts about games and books, but rarely do they make it out of my head and into the world, y'know? So I want to practice writing down my thoughts and critiques more. It's a valuable skill to cultivate and also it's just fun to think critically about the stuff you like (or don't like).
  • I've been waffling back and forth for a few months over a potential rebrand of the site? lunarlily is what I picked when I first made my Neocities account in 2022 but lately I don't know if I really identify with it — it's not a name I use anywhere else. Every time I think about changing to a new domain, though, I end up stressing over making sure all the redirects work and needing a new site button and oh god should I just overhaul the whole design again..?! And then I'm like aughhh whatever I'll worry about it later... thus putting it off indefinitely lol. Maybe I'll come to a decision by the end of another year?
  • My confidence with CSS is at, like, 3 out of 10 on a good day. I want to experiment and learn from the sites I admire! It won't be scary anymore if I just put some time and effort into learning!!
  • Will I finally work up the courage to apply to a webring? Maybe.
  • OK this one is an actual to-do: dark/light mode toggle. I'm putting it here for the sake of accountability.

I thiiink I've rambled over just about everything I wanted to, so! Thank you for taking the time to stop by and read my silly little journals, whether this is your first visit or your hundredth. It's a curiously wonderful feeling to know that there's other humans out there connecting to the things I write... I don't know how to describe it, except that it makes my days a little brighter. Here's to another year of personal websites!
